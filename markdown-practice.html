How To Handle an Unresponsive SME

Working with subject matter experts (SMEs) is key for tech writers working on projects that require specialized knowledge. They provide the crucial details that make your content accurate and high-quality. But when an SME goes silent, it can slow everything down. So, how do you handle it? Here are some tips, plus a personal story of how I worked through resistance from an SME.

What Is an SME, and Why Are They So Important?

A Subject Matter Expert (SME) is someone with deep knowledge and experience in a particular field. They're the go-to person for ensuring your content is accurate and reliable, whether it's a user manual, training guide, or product documentation. As tech writers, we may not be experts in every topic we cover, which is where SMEs come in to save the day.

Why SMEs Are a Writer's Best Friend

Keeping It Accurate: SMEs make sure your writing matches real-world processes or products. A software SME, for example, will guide you through features to ensure your documentation is spot-on.

Filling in the Gaps: When you can't find answers, SMEs provide insights that aren't readily available online.

Double-Checking Everything: SMEs review your content before it's finalized, ensuring it's correct and error-free.

In short, SMEs help turn good content into great content!

Dealing with an Unresponsive SME

Let's face itâ€”SMEs are often juggling many tasks. When they go silent, it can derail your project. But with some strategy and patience, you can keep things moving. Here's how:

  1. Figure Out the Cause: Before jumping to conclusions, try to understand why they're unresponsive. Are they overloaded? Were expectations unclear? Knowing the reason will help you adjust your approach.
  2. Set Clear Expectations: Ensure everyone understands their role, goals, and deadlines. If this wasn't established, send a quick message outlining what you need and by when.
  3. Keep Follow-Ups Simple: Rather than bombarding them with messages, send focused follow-ups that highlight why their input matters. Break tasks into manageable pieces and set realistic deadlines.
  4. Offer Easy Ways to Contribute: Make it easy for them to help. Draft a starting point for them to review, or schedule a quick chat to get answers in real-time.
  5. Be Ready to Pivot: If things don't work out, adjust timelines, loop in a manager, or find alternative sources for the information you need. Flexibility is crucial.

By following these steps, you'll be better prepared to handle a ghosting SME and keep your project on track.

Takeaway Advice

When working with a resistant or ghosting SME, try to understand their perspective while keeping the focus on project goals. If you can't resolve the issue, document your efforts and escalate the problem to minimize disruptions.

Final Thoughts

SMEs are crucial for technical projects, so building a productive relationship with them is essential. Overcoming challenges like unresponsiveness requires empathy and clear communication. By respecting their expertise and time, while focusing on shared goals, you can ensure smoother collaboration and deliver high-quality, user-centered content.
